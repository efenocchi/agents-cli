Layout 8 — Calendar gradient

Same scenario in every cell: Created May 4 · Due May 18 · Today is either May 12 (upcoming) or May 26 (overdue 8d). Four ways to paint the work window + overdue burn-down.

A · Per-cell fade

Each cell in the window gets its own opacity from neon green (created) down to a faint tint (due−1). Due cell has a bright ring. Overdue days ramp from soft red to vivid red as they approach today.

Read me back what I'm proposing
1. Created day — solid neon green (brand color, same as lime LED). The anchor.
2. Days between created & due — green tint that ramps up in intensity as it approaches the due date (D's choice) — or ramps down as time burns through it (A's choice). Two valid metaphors; the prototypes show both. D's "ramp toward due" reads as "pressure building".
3. Due day — same green family, bright ring around the cell. The visual goal post.
4. Past the due day, if today > due (overdue) — that bright ring flips to red, and every day from due → today fills with a red gradient that gets more intense as it nears today. Today itself is a saturated red square with a glow.
5. Days beyond today — neutral. We don't color the unknown future.
